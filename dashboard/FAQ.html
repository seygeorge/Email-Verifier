⭐ What are the meaning of different email verification status?

  • Safe:

    • It's pretty much self-explanatory. Everything is good about that email address, and most likely this is a personal email address.
  • Role:

    • Role addresses are also valid and safe. The only difference is it is a company role-related email address (not a personal one). For example, support@reoon.com (it belongs to the support team, not an individual person).
  • Catch-All:

    • Catch-all emails are not invalid. Catch-all means that the email server accepts emails at any address (even if the individual address does not exist) and most likely redirects them to a specific address that an admin can read. So, if you send emails to these addresses, your email will not bounce even if that individual address does not exist. However, you may receive soft-bounces from them, which are basically a reply email.

  • Disposable:

    • It is a temporary email address. A lot of companies offer temporary email addresses, which are generally valid for about one hour. Sometimes people use these addresses to verify something and after that they never check the email again (also, they will not be able to).

  • Invalid:

    • It means that the email address is not available/registered. Your email will bounce back.

  • Inbox Full:

    • The inbox of that user is full and can no longer receive new emails. However, if he deletes some old emails or increases storage, he can start receiving emails again.

  • Disabled:

    • Similar to invalid. But that account was good before but the provider disabled that. For example, Gmail blocked an account of someone.

  • Unknown:

    • We couldn't verify the status of that individual address (although the server is configured for receiving emails). Your credits are automatically refunded for such results.

⭐ What can cause emails to bounce even if the email address is good/valid/safe?

Emails can bounce for various reasons unrelated to the validity of the email address itself,
including:

    • Incorrect configuration of SPF, rDNS, DKIM, and HELO Commands.

    • Receiving soft bounces, indicating temporary delivery issues but not harming the sender's reputation. Most of the time they are just a reply email.

    • Restrictions on the sender's server IP by email service providers or spam filters.

    • Email content being flagged as spam, potentially due to HTML formatting.

    • Exceeding ISPs' rate limitations.

    • Non-compliance with RFC Standards.

    • Inclusion of 3rd party tracking links that trigger spam filters.

    • Incorrect handling of grey-listed domains by the mailing application

    • Being blocked by the recipient, such as being manually marked as spam.

    • IP and email addresses may not be properly warmed up.

Understanding and addressing these factors can reduce the chances of email bounces, ensuring smoother email communication.

⭐ How can I further reduce email bounces from valid email addresses?

To minimize email bounces from valid addresses, consider the following strategies:

    • Ensure your emails appear natural and limit the number of links and tracking links.

    • Do not send too many emails in short time from one address.

    • Do not include too many links or tracking links.

    • Make sure all links in your emails have a good quality score.

    • Consider using an email warmup service for cold email campaigns.

These tips can help to improve the deliverability of your emails, reducing bounces from good addresses.
